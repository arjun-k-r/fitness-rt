Why is sleep important?

Sleep is more important than diet and eercise combined. Most of our tissue and cellular regeneration and growth occurs during sleep. Our body heals itself, basically, during sleep.

Sleep also adjust our circadian rhythm which is our biological clock. The circadian rhythm dictates all the metabolic reactions the occur on the cellular level.

Sleep is our absorbative state during which all information and nutrients are being accumulated. It is also our most anabolic state, which is the reason why children need so much sleep.

Our mind is also recovering (resting) during sleep, because the brain switches to delta state (deep sleep)

Why is bedtime important?

It is important to have a consistent bedtime so that the body knows how to adjust the circadian rhythm by stimulating the proper hormones at proper times. Also, it is important to catch the 10:00 pm 02:00 window during which the body produces healing and anabolic hormones.

Basically, the more syncronised you sleep, the higher the quality of your sleep and life.

How much should I sleep?

Your sleep runs on cycles of REM and non-REM. REM stands for Rapid Eyed Movement. Each cycle lasts for 90-110 minutes, varying from individual to individual. Basically, you need 5 complete sleep cycles equal to 7-9 hours

Tips for high quality sleep

  • - go to bed and wake up at the same hours;
  • - go to bed before 10:00 pm;
  • - make your room dark like a cave and avoid any source of light before bed, especially electronic devices;
  • - keep the room temperature at around 19°C;
  • - avoid alcohol and caffeine after noon;
  • - leave 3-4 hours between dinner and bedtime;
  • - avoid fatty and sugary foods at dinner;
  • - eat foods high in tryptophan at dinner;
  • - avoid intense exercises in the evening;
  • - relax in the evening.
