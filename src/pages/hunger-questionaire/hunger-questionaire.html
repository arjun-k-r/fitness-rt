Hunger vs. appetite questionaire

Hunger is a physiologic nonspecific drive for ANY food that occurs when the body senses that we need to eat. Appetite, on the other hande. is a psychological desire to consume SPECIFIC foods. It is aroused when environmental cues (i.e. time, place, smell, sight, emotions, thoughts, etc.).

Nowadays, people rarely experience true hunger and commonly experience appetite (cravings) in the absence of hunger which prevents them from making healthy choices.
