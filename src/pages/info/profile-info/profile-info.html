What's my metabolic type?

Each of us has a unique metabolism based on our genes, environment, and lifestyle. As vehicles run on differnt kinds of fuel, your source of energy and nutrient requirements are unqiue.

There are three metabolic types: Protein, Carbo, and Mixed.

Protein types are fast oxidizers or parasympathetic dominant (burn energy very fast and are more chill).

Carbo types are slow oxidizers and sympathetic dominant (burn energy slow and are more alert).

Mixed types are a combination of Protein cand Carbo type.

Your metabolic type doesn't have to be constant. It may change throughout the year according to environmental changes and to your lifestyle. Rememmber to always listen to your body and repeat the test as many times as you feel like.

What's my basal metabolic rate?

Your basal metabolic rate (BMR) is the amount of energy in kilocalories you burn during periods of inactivity. Your BMR is determined using the Revised Harris-Benedict Equation, based on your age, gender, height, and weight. This formula is quite limited, because your BMR is constantly influenced by your lifestyle: sleep, exercise, diet, and your inner state (thoughts and emotions).

What's my body fat percentage?

You percentage of body fat determines the amount of addipose tissue and, by difference, your lean muscle mass your have.

Some addipose tissue is required for optimal health, but too much of everything is bad. Excess body fat and poor lean muscle mass leads to health decline. The ideal body fat percentage is around 13% for men and 24% for women

What's my body shape?

Your body shape is based on the proportions between your chest, waist, and hips (women). Your body shape is an indicator of your overall fitness, especially of your hormonal balance. The ideal shape is the "V shape" for men and the "Hourglass shape" for women. The body shape is influenced by your genes, lifestyle, and hormones.
